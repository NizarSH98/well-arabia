About Well Arabia

Where Heritage
Meets Healing

Across the MENA region, rising rates of diet-related chronic disease coincide with the erosion of centuries-old food traditions. Western dietary models dominate clinical practice, yet they often lack the cultural relevance needed to drive lasting change. The ancestral wisdom that once shaped how entire communities ate, healed, and thrived is at risk of being lost.

Well Arabia exists to reverse that loss. We are a women-led, community-centered platform that scientifically validates, digitizes, and personalizes Arab-Islamic heritage diets — making them accessible to individuals, health professionals, and institutions across the MENA region and the global Arab diaspora.

Our expert team spans business strategy, clinical nutrition, food science, heritage research, and digital communication — united by the conviction that the richest solutions to today's health challenges already exist in the food traditions our grandmothers preserved.

Heritage Food Groups

The Arab Table Model

Ancient Grains
Freekeh, bulgur, barley, and millet — the staple grains of heritage Arab cuisine, rich in fiber and micronutrients.
Legumes & Pulses
Lentils, chickpeas, fava beans, and lupini — protein-dense staples central to daily Arab meals for centuries.
Fermented Foods
Kishek, labneh, preserved lemons, and pickled vegetables — ancestral probiotics that support gut health.
Herbs & Spices
Za'atar, sumac, cumin, turmeric, and black seed — medicinal botanicals with documented health properties.
Oils & Fats
Olive oil, ghee, tahini, and sesame — traditional healthy fats used in cooking, preservation, and remedy.
Seasonal Fruits & Produce
Dates, figs, pomegranates, and seasonal harvests — aligned with traditional agricultural rhythms and fasting cycles.
